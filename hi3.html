--Financial Assets--

Marketable Securities: Easily traded in organized market

Capital Market Security: Earn a regular income and/or capital gain

1. Fixed Income Security: BONDS

Types: Traditional bonds, Callable bonds, convertible bonds, Extendible bonds, Zero coupon bonds, and FLoating rate bonds. Corporate bonds

Corporate bonds- invest rates based on risk, may have positive or negative covenants attached to them. is either secured (colladeral) or debenture (no asset sets aside)

Ratings: by different agencies (i.e. Standard & Poors, Moody's, etc)

2.Equity Security: SHARES

Common shares: shareholders own part of the corporation, get to vote for the Board of Directors, may earn dividends if the corporation earns money (more than preferred shares in order to attract investors), earn capital gains. Shares are rated like bonds, price changed based on demand and expectations.

Preferred shares: shareholders own part of the corporation, earn fixed dividend, earn capital gains. Shares are rated like bonds, price changed base on demand and expectations.

Investment FUND: Debt and/or Equity investments

A fund manager, who must be trained and certified, will invest and manage investor's money after investment objectives are established together, and earn a percentage of the market value of the fund called a management ratio.

Money Market Security

Treasury Bills- low risk, low return

Commercial Paper- issued by large financial institutions with a short maturity

Banker's Acceptance

Derivatives Market Security: A financial contract between two parties for the future urchase or sale of an asset called underlying asset.

The price of a derivative depends on the value of the underlying asset.

Type 1: options:

The holder has the right or option to buy/sell the underlying assets at an agreed price. The writer has the obligation to sell/buy the underlying assets if the holder chooses to.

Exchanged-traded Stock Options: a stock option is written for the trading of 100 shares.

Call option: An option that gives the holder the right to BUY shares from the writer at strike price. The holder expects the market price will be higher than strike price --> in-the-money*.

Buy a Call to speculate: earn premium. Buy a Call to manage risk: guarantee a purchased price.

Put option: An option that gives the holder the right to SELL shares to the writer at strike price. The holder expects the market price will be lower than the strike price --> in-the-money*.

Buy a Put to speculate or manage risk

*In-the-money amount is the intrinsic value of the option

Protected for up to $100,000 per financial institution: Canada Deposit Insurance Coverage (CDIC)
